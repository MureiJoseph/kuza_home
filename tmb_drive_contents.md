# The MANAGER Bridge - TMB (Drive Contents)

## 1. Programme Overview (Main PDF)
- **Title:** The Manager Bridge™ | Leadership Development for New & Aspiring Managers.
- **Core Concept:** A hybrid 8-week leadership programme designed to help new managers transition from peers to leaders.
- **Framework:** Built on the 3-Directional Leadership™ Framework (Leading Down, Leading Up, Leading Across).
- **Key Insight:** Addresses the statistic that 60% of new managers fail within their first 24 months due to a lack of foundational training.
- **Curriculum Pillars:**
  - The Self: Self-awareness and personal leadership philosophy.
  - The Team: High-performance collaboration and delegation.
  - The Results: Strategic execution and driving impact.

## 2. Assessment Results (Folder 0)
- **Target Archetypes:** Focuses heavily on the "Ubuntu Leader" (People-first, relationship-centered, high EQ) and "The Reluctant Authority" (Direct but empathetic, struggles with conflict avoidance).
- **Individual Reports:** Contains detailed leadership profiles for candidates (e.g., Cindy Aluoch, Mary Adongo) which include:
  - Psychometric Analysis: Identifying core strengths and "blind spots."
  - Imposter Syndrome Scores: Quantifying confidence levels in leadership roles.
  - Power Dynamics: Analyzing how individuals feel and exert power within a team.

## 3. Position Document (Folder 1)
- **Introduction:** Contains Module 0 (Welcome), which sets the stage for the cohort journey.
- **Logistics:** Includes the Project Plan 2026, outlining the operational rollout of the programme and key milestones for the year.

## 4. Weekly Modules (Folder 2)
- **Structure:** Organized into Weeks 0 through 8.
- **Week 1 (Sample):** Focuses on "The Self" — developing a "Leadership Creed," understanding EQ, and moving from a "doing" mindset to a "managing" mindset.
- **Core Material:** Each week contains reading materials, exercise prompts, and overview documents to guide the live hybrid sessions.

## 5. Frameworks (Folder 3)

- **3 Circles Framework:** Likely refers to the intersection of Individual, Team, and Task management.
